COVID-19 Dashboard

Introduction

I have multiple travel or group events scheduled over 2020/2021 and have had to cancel many of them. I use as much raw data as possible to inform these cancellation decisions. This dashboard attempts to bring together information I've manually calculated over the past few weeks so it's easier to monitor the situation as other events come closer

Events:

  • Cancelled: March 2020 - Group travel involving British Columbia, California, Illinios, New York, Nevada
  • Cancelled: April 2020 - Large family gathering including group travel involving British Columbia, Alberta, California, Illinios, New York, Taiwan
  • Cancelled: May 2020 - Individual travel involving California, UK, France, Turkey, Spain, Germany
  • At Risk: July 2020 - Individual travel involving British Columbia, California
  • At Risk: Fall 2020 - Group travel involving British Columbia, California, Taiwan
  • At Risk: Spring 2020 - Large family gathering including group travel involving British Columbia, Alberta, California, Illinios, New York, Taiwan

Disclaimer

All data, thoughts, and opinions here are for my personal use only. Be sure to cross check this data with other sources before using it for other purposes. There are bugs and the data is incomplete, inaccurate, and definitely not scientific.

Tips

  • Exceed any government recommendations and pressure them to increase those restrictions. You do not want to play chicken with an exponential graph.
  • Assume media coverage is sensationalizing the wrong things. This situation is simultaneously better and worse than they are reporting. Assume reporters are getting it wrong or asking stupid questions and seek underlying sources yourself.
  • Watch New York Governor Andrew Cuomo's press conferences on YouTube each morning. They are an excellent source of useful data and context that is insightful, direct, and sometimes darkly amusing.
  • Monitor Johns Hopkins worldwide case tracker dashboard for a current macro view.
  • Read this short Washington Post article that shows simple simulation animations of how different containment strategies play out
  • Read this long Medium article on why you must act now, understand Chart 7 in detail, learn how to use death rates to estimate spread, and learn how to convince people in power to act now.
